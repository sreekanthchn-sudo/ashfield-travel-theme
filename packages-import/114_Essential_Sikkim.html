Why Sikkim?

Sikkim is India's hidden Himalayan gem, a pristine mountain state blessed with stunning natural beauty, rich Buddhist heritage, and vibrant cultural diversity. Nestled between Nepal and Bhutan, this five-day journey unveils the serene charm of Gangtok, the breathtaking alpine lakes, and panoramic views of Mount Kanchenjunga, the world's third-highest peak. Explore ancient monasteries, sacred shrines, and lush valleys where prayer flags flutter in the mountain breeze, creating an experience of spiritual tranquility combined with adventure in the Eastern Himalayas.

Your Sikkim Journey

Day 1
DAY 1

Arrive Gangtok

Arrive at Bagdogra Airport or NJP Railway Station. Drive to Gangtok (4-5 hours along the scenic Teesta River, a journey through Himalayan landscapes). In the evening, explore MG Marg, the vibrant pedestrian marketplace, and try local Sikk imese specialties: Thukpa (noodle soup) and Momos (stuffed dumplings).

Hotel: To be confirmed, Gangtok
Meals: None
Day 2
DAY 2

Gangtok Sightseeing

Full day exploring Gangtok's spiritual and cultural landmarks. Visit the peaceful Rumtek Monastery with its ornate prayer halls, the Namgyal Institute of Tibetology housing Buddhist manuscripts and artifacts, sacred Do Drul Chorten stupa, Enchey Monastery perched on a hilltop, and the Flower Exhibition Centre showcasing Sikkim's rare orchids. Take the Gangtok Ropeway cable car for panoramic valley views.

Hotel: Gangtok hotel
Meals: Breakfast
Day 3
DAY 3

Tsomgo Lake and Baba Harbhajan Singh Mandir

Drive to Tsomgo Lake at 12,400 feet, a pristine glacier-fed alpine lake often frozen in winter with spectacular mountain reflections. Continue to the sacred Baba Harbhajan Singh Mandir, a shrine dedicated to an Indian soldier. Optional: Visit Nathu La Pass at the China border (advance permit required). Return to Gangtok, experiencing the full scale of the Eastern Himalayan landscape.

Hotel: Gangtok hotel
Meals: Breakfast
Day 4
DAY 4

Tashi Viewpoint and Gangtok Landmarks

Early morning departure to Tashi Viewpoint for a breathtaking sunrise over Mount Kanchenjunga, the world's third-highest peak. Descend to visit Ganesh Tok and Hanuman Tok, hilltop temples with panoramic views of Gangtok valley. Explore Banjhakri Falls and Park with its scenic waterfall and surrounding gardens. Afternoon shopping at Lal Bazaar for local handicrafts, textiles, and souvenirs.

Hotel: Gangtok hotel
Meals: Breakfast
Day 5
DAY 5

Departure

Enjoy breakfast and depart from your hotel. Drive back to Bagdogra Airport or NJP Railway Station (4-5 hours) for your onward journey. Depart with cherished memories of Sikkim's mountains, monasteries, and warm hospitality.

Departure: Bagdogra Airport or NJP Railway Station
Meals: Breakfast

India Visa & Special Requirements

India Visa Requirements

Most international visitors require a visa to enter India. Citizens of select countries are eligible for e-Visa. Apply through India's official e-Visa portal for tourist visits to Sikkim.

Tourist e-Visa

Valid for 30 or 60 days from the date of issue. Multiple entry options available. Easy online application. Sikkim is accessible under standard tourist e-Visa.

Inner Line Permit

Certain remote areas of Sikkim may require an Inner Line Permit. Most standard tourist areas do not require it. We advise on permits based on your itinerary during booking.

For official visa information, visit the India e-Visa Official Portal. For Nathu La Pass visits, special permits must be arranged in advance.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to visit Sikkim?

March to June offers pleasant weather with clear mountain views, though occasional rain can occur. September to November is ideal with crisp air, blue skies, and excellent visibility of Kanchenjunga. Avoid December-February when temperatures drop significantly and snow closes high-altitude roads.

Can I get altitude sickness?

Tsomgo Lake at 12,400 ft can cause altitude sickness in some visitors. Symptoms include headache, dizziness, and shortness of breath. We recommend ascending gradually, staying hydrated, and taking it easy on Day 3. Most visitors acclimatize well. Consult your doctor before traveling if you have health concerns.

What clothing should I bring?

Pack warm layers including a fleece jacket and windbreaker, especially for early morning mountain visits and evenings. Comfortable walking shoes are essential. In March-June, bring light rain jackets. In September-November, warm clothing for cool mornings and evenings. A hat and sunscreen are recommended for high altitudes.

Do I need a permit for Nathu La Pass?

Yes, Nathu La Pass at the China border requires an advance permit (typically obtained 3-5 days before visit). We can arrange permits as part of your booking. Indian citizens have restrictions. Please discuss this with us during booking if you wish to include this optional visit.

How far is it from Bagdogra to Gangtok?

Approximately 120 kilometers, taking 4-5 hours by road. The scenic drive follows the Teesta River through Himalayan foothills. NJP Railway Station is nearby at similar distance. Transfer is included in your package.

Are the monasteries always open?

Most monasteries are open to visitors, though times may vary. Early morning visits (before 10 AM) often provide serene experiences. Some monasteries close during certain hours for prayers. Our guide will manage timing to ensure meaningful visits at appropriate hours.

Is this trip suitable for all fitness levels?

Most activities involve sightseeing from vehicles and moderate walking. Tsomgo Lake and mountain viewpoints require some climbing and hiking. We recommend reasonable fitness levels. The high altitude may require slower pace. Please inform us of any physical limitations when booking.

Can I try local Sikkimese food?

Yes. MG Marg in Gangtok has excellent restaurants serving authentic Sikkimese cuisine. Thukpa (hot noodle soup) and Momos (dumplings) are must-try specialties. Hotels also serve traditional local and Indian dishes at breakfast and in nearby restaurants. Please inform us of any dietary restrictions.
