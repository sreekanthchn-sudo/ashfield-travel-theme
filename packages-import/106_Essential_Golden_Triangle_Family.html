Why the Golden Triangle?

The Golden Triangle is India's most iconic family-friendly travel route, connecting three magnificent imperial cities that captivate both young and old with their splendour, history, and wonder. This six-day family journey unveils the grandeur of the Mughal Empire through curious eyes — from the walled streets of Old Delhi to the marble perfection of the Taj Mahal and the palatial majesty of Jaipur. Your children will love exploring ancient forts, watching peacocks in gardens, discovering bazaars, and creating lifelong memories while learning about centuries of extraordinary architecture and culture.

Your Golden Triangle Family Journey

Day 1
DAY 1

Arrive Delhi

Arrive at Delhi Airport where you'll be warmly greeted and transferred to your family-friendly hotel in your private AC vehicle. Rest of the day at leisure for the children to settle in and relax after travel.

Hotel: Park Inn by Radisson, Delhi
Meals: None
Day 2
DAY 2

Delhi Sightseeing

Discover Old Delhi's treasures at a family pace: Red Fort with its grand gates and courtyards, Rajghat (Gandhi's peaceful memorial), India Gate (perfect for photos), Rashtrapati Bhawan, Humayun's Tomb with beautiful gardens, spiritual Lotus Temple, towering Qutub Minar, and Birla Mandir. Children enjoy the mix of history, spirituality, and beautiful spaces.

Hotel: Park Inn by Radisson, Delhi
Meals: Breakfast
Day 3
DAY 3

Delhi to Agra

Drive to Agra (210km/4hrs) in your private, comfortable AC Toyota Crysta with plenty of stops for stretching and snacks. Visit the breathtaking Taj Mahal — a magical experience for the whole family. Explore the imposing Agra Fort with its stories of emperors and grandeur. Note: Taj Mahal is closed on Fridays.

Hotel: Royal Sarover Portico, Agra
Meals: Breakfast
Day 4
DAY 4

Agra to Jaipur via Fatehpur Sikri

Travel to Jaipur (240km/5-6hrs) with a fascinating en route stop at Fatehpur Sikri, the perfectly preserved Mughal ghost city where children's imaginations come alive. Arrive in Jaipur and explore vibrant, colourful markets by traditional cycle rickshaw in the evening — a fun, immersive experience for the whole family.

Hotel: Nirbana Palace, Jaipur
Meals: Breakfast
Day 5
DAY 5

Jaipur Sightseeing

Explore Jaipur's iconic landmarks at a relaxed family pace: Amber Fort with elephant rides (optional), stunning architecture, and panoramic views; Nahargarh Fort; the opulent City Palace; astronomical Jantar Mantar (fascinating for curious minds); the intricate Hawa Mahal with its 953 small windows; and spiritual Birla Temple. Afternoon at leisure for family relaxation or shopping.

Hotel: Nirbana Palace, Jaipur
Meals: Breakfast
Day 6
DAY 6

Departure

Drive to Delhi or Jaipur Airport (260km/5.5hrs from Jaipur if Delhi) for your onward flight. Depart with precious family memories of India's most magnificent monuments and a deeper appreciation of Mughal heritage that the children will treasure for years.

Departure: Delhi or Jaipur Airport
Meals: Breakfast

India Visa Information

Visa Requirements

Most international visitors require a visa to enter India. Citizens of select countries are eligible for e-Visa, which can be obtained online. Children require their own visas.

Tourist Visa (e-Visa)

Valid for 30 or 60 days from the date of issue. Multiple entries available. Easy online application through India's official e-Visa portal for all family members.

Processing Time

e-Visa typically approved within 3-4 business days. We recommend applying at least 2 weeks before your departure date for the whole family.

For official visa information and eligibility, visit the India e-Visa Official Portal

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Golden Triangle suitable for families with young children?

Absolutely! The Golden Triangle is one of India's most family-friendly routes. Our itinerary is paced for families, with a private vehicle, spacious family rooms, excellent hotels, and sightseeing activities that engage children of all ages. The mix of monuments, gardens, and cultural experiences keeps everyone entertained and educated.

How comfortable is the driving for children?

Our private AC Toyota Crysta is spacious and comfortable, perfect for families. The air-conditioning, comfortable seating, and smooth suspension make long drives pleasant. We build in regular stops for stretching, snacks, and restroom breaks. Delhi-Agra is 4 hours; Agra-Jaipur is 5-6 hours with a scenic stop at Fatehpur Sikri.

What's the best age to bring children?

Children aged 6+ get the most from the experience — they can appreciate the monuments and engage with guides. However, younger children (2-5 years) can enjoy the journey, gardens, and family bonding. Infants below 2 years travel free. Tailor activities to your child's interests and energy levels.

What food options are available for children?

All hotels offer diverse breakfast options. Most serve fresh fruit, cereals, toast, eggs, and Indian dishes. For lunch and dinner, we can recommend family-friendly restaurants with mild, familiar options. Indian food is generally mild in the north; spice levels can be adjusted. Pack familiar snacks if your children prefer them.

What's the best time to visit with children?

October to March is ideal for families, offering pleasant temperatures (15-30°C), clear skies, and comfortable sightseeing conditions. Avoid the peak period of 15 Dec to 15 Jan due to higher costs and crowds. April to September brings intense heat (40°C+) and monsoon rains, making family travel challenging.

Are there elephant rides available in Jaipur?

Elephant rides at Amber Fort are optional and available for an additional fee. Children must be old enough to sit safely (typically 4+ years) and comfortable with the experience. This can be arranged as an add-on. We also offer alternative activities if your family prefers not to participate in animal interactions.

Is the Taj Mahal always open?

The Taj Mahal is closed on Fridays and certain national holidays. Our itinerary is designed to accommodate this. Sunrise and sunset visits are particularly magical for families and highly recommended. The experience is unforgettable for children.

Are there any health or safety concerns for children?

India is generally safe for families. Standard precautions apply: drink bottled water, use sun protection, and carry basic medications. Hotels meet international standards. Delhi's air quality can be a concern — consider bringing masks during peak pollution months. Consult your doctor before travel regarding vaccinations and malaria prevention.

What should children wear?

During October-March, bring light layers for warm days and a light jacket for cooler evenings. Comfortable, breathable clothing is essential. Comfortable walking shoes are a must for monument exploration. At religious sites, modest clothing (shoulders and knees covered) is respectful. Sun hats and sunscreen are highly recommended.

Are monument entrance fees included?

No, entrance fees are not included in the base package. However, we offer a convenient add-on covering all major monument entries for INR 8,800 per person (approximately £88). Note: Children under 5 are often free at major sites, and discounts may apply for 5-12 year-olds.

Can we add a local guide for children's benefit?

Yes. English-speaking guides are available as an add-on at £35 per day. We highly recommend guides for Delhi, Agra (Taj Mahal), and Jaipur — they make history come alive for children with engaging stories and age-appropriate explanations. Guides can be tailored to your children's ages and interests.
