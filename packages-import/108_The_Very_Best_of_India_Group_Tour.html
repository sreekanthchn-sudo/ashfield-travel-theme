Your Very Best of India Journey

GOLDEN TRIANGLE LEG (Days 1-5): Jaipur · Agra · Delhi

Experience India's most iconic monuments and imperial cities before your internal flight to Cochin.

Day 1
DAY 1

Arrive Jaipur

Arrive at Jaipur Airport and transfer to your hotel via luxury AC coach. Afternoon and evening at leisure. Begin your sightseeing with visits to the magnificent Amber Fort, opulent City Palace, astronomical Jantar Mantar, iconic Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds), and serene Jal Mahal (Water Palace).

Hotel: Ramada Wyndham Raja Park, Jaipur
Meals: Lunch, Dinner
Day 2
DAY 2

Full Day Jaipur Sightseeing

Discover Jaipur's remaining landmarks: impressive Jaigarh Fort with historic cannons, Nahargarh Fort with panoramic city views, Albert Hall Museum, and Monkey Temple. Explore vibrant bazaars offering local crafts, textiles, and traditional Jaipur prints. Evening at leisure to rest and prepare for your journey.

Hotel: Ramada Wyndham Raja Park, Jaipur
Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Day 3
DAY 3

Jaipur to Agra

Drive to Agra (240km/5-6 hours) in your AC 35-seater luxury coach. Visit the breathtaking Taj Mahal and the imposing Agra Fort, witnessing the romance and grandeur of Mughal architecture. Experience the perfect blend of love, history, and artistic magnificence. Note: Taj Mahal is closed on Fridays.

Hotel: Radisson Agra
Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Day 4
DAY 4

Agra to Delhi

Drive to Delhi (206km/4-5 hours) in your luxury coach. Afternoon and evening sightseeing includes the magnificent Red Fort, Rajghat (Mahatma Gandhi's memorial), iconic India Gate, historic Jama Masjid, and the architectural marvel of Akshardham Temple. A comprehensive journey through centuries of Indian history and heritage.

Hotel: The Metropolitan, Delhi
Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Day 5
DAY 5

Internal Flight: Delhi to Cochin

Transfer to Delhi Airport for your internal flight to Cochin (approximately 4 hours). Arrive in Cochin and transfer to your hotel. Evening leisure at Fort Cochin. Optional evening visit to traditional Kathakali dance show (evening classical Indian dance-drama with elaborate costumes and makeup).

Hotel: Tower House / Fragrant Nature Kochi
Meals: Breakfast, Dinner

KERALA LEG (Days 6-11): Cochin · Munnar · Alleppey · Marari · Kovalam

Discover South India's enchanting landscapes, from hill stations and spice plantations to backwater cruises and tropical beaches.

Day 6
DAY 6

Cochin Sightseeing · Drive to Munnar

Morning sightseeing in Cochin: explore the Dutch Palace with its intricate murals, watch iconic Chinese Fishing Nets operating at the waterfront, and visit the historic Paradesi Synagogue. Then drive to Munnar (140km/4 hours), India's premier hill station nestled in the Western Ghats. Arrive and settle into your resort in the misty mountains.

Hotel: Fragrant Nature Resort, Munnar
Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Day 7
DAY 7

Full Day Munnar Sightseeing

Explore Munnar's natural and agricultural wonders: visit Mattupetty Dam for stunning valley views, discover Echo Point for its acoustic phenomena, explore Rajamala sanctuary for Nilgiri tahr sightings, and tour a working spice garden to see cardamom, pepper, and cinnamon plantations. Experience the cool mountain air and lush green landscapes at their finest.

Hotel: Fragrant Nature Resort, Munnar
Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Day 8
DAY 8

Munnar to Alleppey · Houseboat Cruise · Marari Beach

Drive to Alleppey (170km/5 hours), Kerala's backwater capital. Board a luxury AC houseboat for a memorable day cruise through serene palm-fringed lagoons, canals, and paddy fields. Experience the tranquility of India's Venice, watching local life unfold along the waterways. Lunch served on the houseboat. Afternoon transfer to Marari Beach and check into your beachfront resort.

Hotel: Xandari Pearl Resort, Marari
Meals: Breakfast, Lunch (houseboat), Dinner
Day 9
DAY 9

Marari to Kovalam Beach

Drive to Kovalam (160km/4 hours), passing through Trivandrum, Kerala's capital. En route, brief sightseeing includes Trivandrum's historic temples and landmarks. Arrive at Kovalam and check into your beachfront resort. Afternoon and evening free to relax on the golden sandy beaches and experience the Arabian Sea.

Hotel: Gokulam Grand Turtle Beach Resort, Kovalam
Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Day 10
DAY 10

Full Day Leisure at Kovalam

Spend a complete day relaxing at Kovalam Beach. Enjoy swimming, beach walks, sunbathing, and local beach activities. Optional water sports available (parasailing, jet skiing) at additional cost. Unwind after your comprehensive India journey and savour the tropical paradise.

Hotel: Gokulam Grand Turtle Beach Resort, Kovalam
Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Day 11
DAY 11

Departure from Trivandrum

Transfer to Trivandrum International Airport for your onward journey. Depart with unforgettable memories of India's greatest treasures: Mughal monuments, mountain landscapes, spice gardens, serene backwaters, and pristine beaches. Shared with fellow travellers who became friends.

Departure: Trivandrum Airport
Meals: Breakfast

India Visa Information

Visa Requirements

Most international visitors require a visa to enter India. Citizens of select countries are eligible for e-Visa, which can be obtained online for both entry and exit points.

Tourist Visa (e-Visa)

Valid for 30 or 60 days from the date of issue. Multiple entries available. Easy online application through India's official e-Visa portal.

Processing Time

e-Visa typically approved within 3-4 business days. We recommend applying at least 2 weeks before your departure date.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's included in this combined package?

The Very Best of India combines two perfectly matched group tours: the Golden Triangle (Jaipur, Agra, Delhi) for 5 days, plus Kerala (Cochin, Munnar, backwaters, beaches) for 6 days. You get complete North and South India experiences in one journey. The internal flight Delhi-Cochin is NOT included and must be booked separately.

How do I arrange the internal flight Delhi to Cochin?

The internal flight is a separate component not included in the package price. Our team can assist with flight recommendations and bookings, but you arrange it independently. The flight is approximately 4 hours. We recommend a mid-morning flight to allow time for hotel checkout and airport transfer.

What's the best time to visit?

October to March is ideal for North India, offering pleasant temperatures (15-30°C) and clear skies. This same period is perfect for Kerala (25-35°C, dry season). This tour specifically departs in January when weather is optimal throughout both regions. April to September brings intense heat and monsoon rains in Kerala.

How many people are in the group?

Maximum 26 guests per departure. This ensures a balanced group experience with personalized attention while maintaining group enjoyment, camaraderie, and convenience. Friendships often develop naturally on these extended journeys.

Are monument entrance fees included?

No, entrance fees are not included in the base package. However, we offer a convenient add-on covering major monuments (Taj Mahal, Agra Fort, Red Fort, city temples), which you can book when confirming your package. This allows flexibility for optional sites.

What are the coach changes?

You travel in an AC 35-seater coach for the Golden Triangle leg (Days 1-4), then switch to an AC 34-seater coach for the Kerala leg (Days 5-11). Both are luxury, air-conditioned, and professionally maintained with experienced drivers.

Is the houseboat cruise included?

Yes. The full-day luxury AC houseboat cruise on Alleppey backwaters (Day 8) is fully included, with lunch served on board. This is one of the highlights of the Kerala leg.

What should I wear?

January temperatures vary: North India (15-25°C) requires light layers; Kerala (25-35°C) is warm with high humidity. Bring comfortable walking shoes for monuments, light breathable clothes for Kerala, and a light jacket for evening. Modest clothing (shoulders, knees covered) is respectful at religious sites.

How much walking is involved?

Moderate to extensive. Monument exploration involves considerable walking with some stairs. The houseboat cruise is relaxed. Beach time is leisurely. Overall fitness level should be moderate. Our guides adjust pace for the group's comfort.

Are there opportunities to meet local people?

Absolutely. Bazaar visits, spice garden tours, houseboat interactions with local boatmen, and beach communities offer authentic cultural encounters. Your guide facilitates respectful interactions and provides cultural context throughout.
