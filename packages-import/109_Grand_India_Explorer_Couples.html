Journey Overview

The Grand India Explorer combines the best of India's cultural heartland with the serene backwaters of Kerala. This 13-day couples tour takes you from the iconic monuments of the Golden Triangle—Delhi, Agra, and Jaipur—to the lush tea plantations of Munnar, the tranquil lakes of Kumarakom, and the sun-kissed beaches of Marari. Experience royal palaces, ancient temples, sacred rivers, vibrant culture, and authentic hospitality in handpicked premium hotels.

Perfect for couples seeking a complete Indian immersion, this itinerary blends history, nature, spirituality, and relaxation. Travel in private air-conditioned vehicles with professional English-speaking guides, enjoy daily breakfasts, and savour a traditional houseboat lunch in the backwaters. This is India at its most romantic and rewarding.

Highlights

Taj Mahal, Agra

Taj Mahal

Marvel at the world's greatest symbol of love. A UNESCO World Heritage Site and timeless monument to devotion, the Taj Mahal is an unforgettable highlight of any Indian journey.

Amber Fort, Jaipur

Amber Fort

Perched on a hilltop overlooking Jaipur, this magnificent fort showcases Rajasthani and Mughal architecture. Explore mirrored chambers, courtyards, and panoramic views.

Fort Cochin

Fort Cochin

Wander through colonial streets lined with Portuguese villas, spice warehouses, and iconic Chinese fishing nets. A living museum of Kerala's maritime heritage.

Munnar Tea Country

Munnar Tea Plantations

Surrounded by rolling tea gardens and misty mountains, Munnar is Kerala's premier hill station. Walk among endless tea bushes and meet local plantation workers.

Alleppey Backwaters

Alleppey Houseboat Cruise

Drift silently through palm-fringed lagoons on a traditional houseboat. Enjoy lunch, wave to villages, and witness Kerala's unique way of life from the water.

Marari Beach

Marari Beach Retreat

Relax on pristine sands kissed by the Arabian Sea. Experience village life, enjoy beach walks, and unwind at a secluded beachfront resort before departure.

Complete Itinerary

Day 1: Arrive Delhi

Arrive at Delhi Airport. Transfer to hotel. Leisure time to settle in and rest after your journey. Overnight in the capital.
Hotel: Park Inn by Radisson, Delhi (Premium) | The Metropoliton (Luxury)
Meals: None

Day 2: Delhi Sightseeing

Full-day guided tour of Delhi's iconic landmarks. Visit Red Fort (UNESCO World Heritage Site), Rajghat (Mahatma Gandhi's memorial), India Gate, Humayun's Tomb, Lotus Temple, Qutub Minar, and Birla Mandir. Witness the blend of Mughal and modern Indian architecture. Evening leisure.
Hotel: Park Inn by Radisson, Delhi (Premium) | The Metropoliton (Luxury)
Meals: Breakfast

Day 3: Delhi to Agra

Drive to Agra (210km / 4 hours). Afternoon tour of the Taj Mahal, the world's greatest monument to love and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Later, explore Agra Fort, a massive red sandstone fortress with palace ruins and gardens.
Hotel: Royal Sarover Portico, Agra (Premium) | Radisson, Agra (Luxury)
Meals: Breakfast

Day 4: Agra to Jaipur via Fatehpur Sikri

Drive to Jaipur (240km / 5-6 hours), stopping en route at Fatehpur Sikri, the abandoned Mughal capital. Explore the stunning Palace Complex, Buland Darwaza (Gate of Magnificence), and Jama Masjid. Continue to Jaipur and settle into your hotel.
Hotel: Nirbana Palace, Jaipur (Premium) | Radisson Blu, Jaipur (Luxury)
Meals: Breakfast

Day 5: Jaipur Sightseeing

Explore Jaipur, the "Pink City." Visit Amber Fort (hilltop palace with stunning views), City Palace (still partially inhabited by the royal family), Jantar Mantar (astronomical observation site, UNESCO World Heritage), Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds), and Birla Temple. Evening leisure.
Hotel: Nirbana Palace, Jaipur (Premium) | Radisson Blu, Jaipur (Luxury)
Meals: Breakfast

Day 6: Jaipur to Cochin (Internal Flight)

Morning breakfast at Jaipur hotel. Drive to Jaipur Airport. Fly to Cochin (internal flight, not included in package price). Transfer to hotel. Evening: experience a traditional Kathakali dance performance, Kerala's ancient classical art form.
Hotel: Abad Dutch Bungalow, Cochin (Premium) | Tower House, Cochin (Luxury)
Meals: Breakfast (at Jaipur hotel)

Day 7: Cochin to Munnar

Morning sightseeing in Cochin. Visit the Dutch Palace (former Portuguese Governor's residence), Chinese Fishing Nets (iconic backwater landmark), and Jewish Synagogue. Drive to Munnar (140km / 4 hours), ascending into misty tea country. Settle into your hill station resort.
Hotel: The Fog Resort, Munnar (Premium) | Fragrant Nature Resort, Munnar (Luxury)
Meals: Breakfast

Day 8: Munnar Sightseeing

Full-day guided tour of Munnar. Visit Mattupetty Dam and lake, Echo Point (spectacular valley views), Rajamala (wildlife sanctuary), and a local spice garden where you'll learn about cardamom, pepper, vanilla, and other plantation crops. Enjoy the cool mountain air and lush landscapes.
Hotel: The Fog Resort, Munnar (Premium) | Fragrant Nature Resort, Munnar (Luxury)
Meals: Breakfast

Day 9: Munnar to Kumarakom

Drive to Kumarakom (150km / 4 hours), descending from the hills to the lakeside. Kumarakom is famous for its backwater birds and serene atmosphere. Visit Vembanad Lake and the bird sanctuary. Relax and enjoy the tranquil surroundings.
Hotel: Lake Song Resort, Kumarakom (Premium) | Rhythm Resort, Kumarakom (Luxury)
Meals: Breakfast

Day 10: Alleppey Houseboat Cruise & Marari Beach

All-day houseboat cruise in Alleppey. Board a traditional AC houseboat and drift through palm-fringed backwater lagoons. Enjoy lunch (included) on the houseboat while observing village life, coconut groves, and local culture. Afternoon: transfer to Marari Beach resort (70km / 2 hours). Evening leisure on the beach.
Hotel: Abad Turtle Beach Resort, Marari (Premium) | Xandari Beach Resort, Marari (Luxury)
Meals: Breakfast, Lunch (houseboat)

Day 11: Marari Beach Leisure

Full day to relax at this pristine, uncrowded beach. Optional village walk to meet locals, explore traditional fishing villages, enjoy the sunset, or simply relax under swaying palms. Beach activities and spa treatments available at the resort.
Hotel: Abad Turtle Beach Resort, Marari (Premium) | Xandari Beach Resort, Marari (Luxury)
Meals: Breakfast

Day 12: Marari to Cochin & Spa

Drive to Cochin (70km / 2 hours). Check into hotel. Afternoon: enjoy Ayurvedic spa treatment or traditional massage at a heritage Ayurveda centre. Evening leisure in the historic port city.
Hotel: Abad Dutch Bungalow, Cochin (Premium) | Tower House, Cochin (Luxury)
Meals: Breakfast

Day 13: Depart Cochin

Transfer to Cochin Airport for departure. Fly home with memories of India's magnificent Golden Triangle and serene Kerala backwaters.
Meals: Breakfast

Visa & Travel Information

India Visa Requirements

Most UK citizens require a visa to enter India. We recommend applying for an e-Visa (online) for convenience, which typically takes 3–5 working days to process. Tourist visas allow 60 days and are suitable for this itinerary.

E-Visa Process:

  • Apply online at the official Indian immigration portal
  • Provide a scanned passport, photograph, and basic information
  • Processing: 3–5 business days
  • Cost: approximately £10–15 GBP
  • Visa approval is sent by email; print and carry it with your passport

Alternative: Apply for a traditional visa through the Indian High Commission in London (longer processing, but valid for multiple entries).

Health & Vaccinations

We recommend consulting your doctor at least 4–6 weeks before departure. Common recommendations include:

  • Typhoid protection
  • Hepatitis A vaccine
  • Routine immunisations (MMR, tetanus)
  • Malaria precautions (if visiting during monsoon months)
  • Travel insurance with medical cover

Travel Insurance

Comprehensive travel insurance is essential. Ensure it covers medical emergencies, trip cancellation, and evacuation. India has excellent private hospitals in major cities, but insurance provides peace of mind.

Best Time to Visit

Season: October to March (excluding 15 December – 15 January for holiday pricing). This period offers pleasant temperatures across India—cool mornings in the north, perfect for sightseeing, and mild evenings in the south.

Climate & What to Pack

Clothing: Light cotton clothing for warm days, a light jacket for evenings (especially in Munnar), comfortable walking shoes, hat, and sunscreen.

Luggage: Soft luggage is recommended (easier to manoeuvre in hotels and vehicles). Allow extra space for shopping.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the internal flight included in the price?
No. The internal flight from Jaipur (or Delhi) to Cochin on Day 6 is not included. We can assist with flight booking or you may arrange your own. Budget approximately £60–150 per person for this leg.
What is the difference between Premium and Luxury pricing?
Premium includes 4-star hotels in comfort categories (e.g., Park Inn by Radisson). Luxury features 5-star deluxe properties (e.g., The Metropoliton, Radisson Blu) with enhanced amenities, finer dining, and spa facilities. Both tiers include the same itinerary and experiences.
Can I adjust the itinerary or extend the tour?
Yes. We customise all tours. You may extend in Kerala, add Goa, visit Mumbai, or modify destinations. Contact us to discuss options and pricing adjustments.
Do I need to book the internal flight in advance?
We recommend booking 2–3 weeks before departure for better rates and seat availability. Budget airlines and major carriers operate this route regularly. Contact us for booking assistance.
How many people travel together in the vehicle?
You'll have a private air-conditioned Toyota Crysta dedicated to your couple. The vehicle is not shared with other travellers. The Golden Triangle and Kerala legs use separate vehicles as they are in different regions.
What meals are provided?
Daily breakfast is included at all hotels. Lunch and dinner are at your leisure (we recommend local restaurants with guide assistance). The houseboat lunch on Day 10 is included. We can arrange cooking classes or restaurant reservations upon request.
Is the Kathakali show included?
Yes. A traditional Kathakali dance performance in Cochin is included on Day 6 evening. Kathakali is Kerala's ancient classical art form—highly recommended for couples.
What about golden triangle monument entrance fees?
Entrance fees for major monuments (Taj Mahal, Agra Fort, Amber Fort, etc.) are not included but available as a paid add-on. Typically costs £30–50 per person. Guides will accompany you; these are structured to ensure you experience each monument fully.
Is travel insurance required?
Not mandatory, but highly recommended. Insurance should cover medical emergencies, trip cancellation, and evacuation. India has excellent hospitals in major cities, but insurance provides security and peace of mind.
What is the payment schedule?
Typically: 30% deposit on booking confirmation, 70% balance 6 weeks before departure. Flexible payment plans available upon request. Full cancellation policy provided at booking.
How do I book, and what's your cancellation policy?
Contact us via email or phone to confirm availability and receive a detailed quote. A contract with cancellation terms will be provided. Generally: cancellations 60+ days before = 20% retention; 30–60 days = 50%; less than 30 days = non-refundable. Travel insurance often covers cancellations.
Can you arrange a romantic setup (flowers, champagne, etc.)?
Absolutely. We love enhancing romantic moments. Let us know in advance and we'll arrange surprise elements—special room setup, champagne on arrival, romantic dining, spa treatments. Additional cost may apply.
