Why Kerala for Families?

Kerala, India's southernmost jewel, is an ideal destination for families seeking an enchanting blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and child-friendly adventures. With its mist-draped mountains, emerald-green tea plantations, tranquil backwater networks, and pristine beaches, Kerala offers a landscape of wonder that captures children's imaginations whilst parents relax. This 8-day journey has been thoughtfully crafted to showcase the very best of God's Own Country, from the colonial charm of Fort Cochin to the serene beauty of the Alleppey Backwaters and the golden sands of Marari Beach—all at a pace perfect for families with young children.

Every moment of your Kerala family adventure is supported by Ashfield Travel's expertise and local knowledge. We've handpicked family-friendly hotels with spacious rooms, arranged private transport where children can move freely, and curated experiences designed specifically for families. Whether your children are discovering life on Kerala's backwaters, exploring tea plantations, or building sandcastles on pristine beaches, this is a journey tailored to create memories that your whole family will treasure for years to come.

Your Day-by-Day Journey

Cochin Airport Arrival
DAY 1

Arrive in Cochin

Welcome to Kerala! Upon arrival at Cochin Airport, you'll be met and transferred to your family-friendly hotel in Fort Cochin. Settle into your spacious room and spend the rest of the day at leisure, allowing your children to adjust to the new environment. Explore the atmospheric streets and colonial buildings of this historic quarter at a relaxed pace. In the evening, immerse your family in Kerala's rich cultural heritage with a private Kathakali dance performance—a UNESCO-recognised classical art form featuring elaborate costumes, intricate facial expressions, and mesmerising movements that captivate audiences of all ages.

Hotel: Abad Dutch Bungalow
Meals: None
Cochin Sightseeing & Drive to Munnar
DAY 2

Cochin Sightseeing & Drive to Munnar

After breakfast, embark on a family-paced sightseeing tour of Cochin with your personal guide. Visit the iconic Dutch Palace with its ancient murals, the centuries-old Jewish Synagogue, the famous Chinese Fishing Nets that have graced Kerala's shores for centuries, and the historic St Francis Church. Your children will enjoy watching the traditional fishing nets in action. In the afternoon, begin your scenic drive to Munnar (140 km, approximately 4 hours) through winding roads adorned with waterfalls and cascading tea plantations. Lunch is at a local restaurant (own expense). Arrive in the cooler hill station of Munnar and settle into your family retreat for the evening.

Hotel: The Fog Resort, Munnar
Meals: Breakfast
Distance: 140 km / 4 hrs
Munnar Tea Plantations
DAY 3

Munnar Sightseeing

Spend a full day exploring the breathtaking beauty of Munnar with your English-speaking guide. Visit the picturesque Mattupetty Dam, where the Kundali River creates a stunning reservoir set against verdant hills. Experience the panoramic views from Echo Point, where sounds echo dramatically across the valleys—children love this natural phenomenon. Climb to Top Station, Munnar's highest viewpoint, offering sweeping vistas of endless tea plantations rolling across the hillsides. Venture into Rajamala, part of the Eravikulam National Park, a sanctuary for the endangered Nilgiri Tahr and other alpine species. Finally, tour a working spice garden to learn about the cultivation of cardamom, pepper, and cloves at 1,600 metres above sea level. Return to your hotel as the misty evening descends, perfect for a family dinner and early rest.

Hotel: The Fog Resort, Munnar
Meals: Breakfast
Kumarakom Lake & Bird Sanctuary
DAY 4

Munnar to Kumarakom

After breakfast, drive to Kumarakom (150 km, approximately 4 hours), a serene destination nestled on the banks of Vembanad Lake—Kerala's biggest and longest freshwater lake. Check into your lakeside resort, which offers plenty of space for children to explore safely. Spend the afternoon at the Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary, a paradise for young birdwatchers and nature lovers. The sanctuary is home to over 200 species of migratory and resident birds, including Siberian cranes, herons, egrets, and kingfishers. Your guide will lead your family through the wetlands as the golden afternoon light dances across the water, creating natural learning moments about wildlife conservation.

Hotel: Lake Song Resort, Kumarakom
Meals: Breakfast
Distance: 150 km / 4 hrs
Alleppey Houseboat Day Cruise
DAY 5

Alleppey Day Cruise & Transfer to Marari Beach

After breakfast, drive to Alleppey (50 km, approximately 1.5 hours), the gateway to Kerala's famous backwaters. Board your private air-conditioned houseboat for an unforgettable family day cruise through the intricate network of lagoons, lakes, and canals that define this unique landscape. Children love spotting traditional fishermen, wooden canoes, and village life from the deck. You'll be greeted with a welcome drink and spend the day drifting past coconut palms, spice plantations, and traditional fishing villages. A sumptuous lunch, prepared by your private chef on board, features authentic Kerala cuisine with fresh seafood and local specialities that appeal to all palates. Enjoy evening tea and snacks as you cruise through the golden sunset hour. Disembark at 4:00 PM, then transfer to Marari Beach Resort (30 km, approximately 1 hour), where you'll check in to your beachfront accommodation as the sun dips below the horizon.

Hotel: Abad Turtle Beach Resort, Marari
Meals: Breakfast, Lunch (on houseboat)
Distance: 50 km + 30 km
Marari Beach Resort
DAY 6

Marari Beach

Dedicate an entire day to relaxation and family fun at Marari Beach, one of Kerala's most pristine and unspoilt coastal destinations. The beach is renowned for its soft white sand, gentle waves, and the authentic atmosphere of a working fishing community. This is your opportunity to unwind whilst your children enjoy safe swimming, sandcastle building, and beach exploration. Optional family-friendly activities include visiting a local Hindu temple with its ornate gopuram and spiritual atmosphere, exploring nearby coir farms where coconut fibre is processed using traditional methods, observing how local fishermen work, or wandering through a small fishing village to witness daily life. Enjoy cycling along the beach paths as a family, watch the spectacular sunset paint the sky in shades of orange and gold, and experience the unhurried pace of coastal Kerala life. Return to your resort for dinner with a sea breeze and the sound of the ocean as your soundtrack.

Hotel: Abad Turtle Beach Resort, Marari
Meals: Breakfast
Return to Cochin
DAY 7

Marari to Cochin

After breakfast, drive back to Cochin (70 km, approximately 2 hours) for your final night in Kerala. Check into your hotel and spend the afternoon at leisure. This is an ideal time for your family to relax by the pool, indulge in a rejuvenating wellness treatment at your hotel's spa, or let the children play whilst parents unwind. The evening is yours to savour Kerala's culinary delights; the streets of Fort Cochin are lined with excellent family-friendly restaurants and cafes serving everything from traditional Kerala fish curry to contemporary cuisine. Take a final walk through the colonial quarter as a family, perhaps revisiting a favourite spot or discovering a hidden gem you missed earlier in the week.

Hotel: Abad Dutch Bungalow, Cochin
Meals: Breakfast
Distance: 70 km / 2 hrs
Departure from Cochin
DAY 8

Departure from Cochin

After breakfast, transfer to Cochin International Airport for your onward flight. As you fly home, reflect on the unforgettable journey through God's Own Country. The memories of mist-covered mountains, serene backwaters, pristine beaches, and the warmth of Kerala's hospitality—and the joy on your children's faces—will remain with you long after you've returned home.

Indian Visa Information

Who Does This Apply To?

British passport holders can apply for an Indian e-Visa online, which is the most convenient option for UK citizens travelling to India for tourism purposes. The e-Visa is a digital visa issued electronically and does not require a physical stamp in your passport. Children must have their own passports and visa applications.

How to Apply

Visit indianvisaonline.gov.in/evisa/tvoa.html to apply for your Indian e-Visa. Apply no earlier than 30 days before your date of arrival; we recommend applying at least 28 days in advance to allow processing time. Ensure your passport is valid for at least 6 months from your date of travel and has at least 2 blank pages. Each family member will need to apply separately.

Indian Reference Contact

When applying for your e-Visa, you may be asked to provide an Indian reference contact. If needed, please contact us and we can assist with providing the necessary details for your application.

Need Help with Your Visa?

While the online e-Visa process is straightforward, professional visa assistance is available through The Travel Visa Company. Please note that third-party visa fees apply if you choose this service. We recommend applying yourself first, as it is usually quicker and more cost-effective.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Kerala suitable for young children?

Yes, Kerala is very family-friendly. Our package is designed for families with children aged 2–12 years. The pace is relaxed, hotels are spacious and well-equipped, and all activities are child-appropriate. The private transport allows flexibility for naps and breaks. We do not recommend this package for very young infants (below 2 years) due to travel distances, though we can make special arrangements upon request.

What happens if a child gets ill during the trip?

Your hotel can arrange for a doctor to visit your room if needed. There are also private hospitals in Cochin and Munnar with English-speaking doctors. We strongly recommend comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical expenses. Please inform us of any medical conditions or allergies when booking so we can make appropriate arrangements.

Are the hotels child-friendly?

Yes, all our selected hotels offer family-friendly amenities including spacious rooms, children's menus, pool facilities, and babysitting services upon request. Hotels are accustomed to families with children and can provide extra beds or cots. We recommend informing us of your children's ages when booking so we can request appropriate room configurations.

What is the drive time between destinations?

Drive times are 4–5 hours between major destinations. The private air-conditioned car provides comfort, and we schedule breaks for refreshments and stretching. Our driver is experienced in family travel and can adjust speeds for comfort. It's normal for children to nap during travel in Kerala.

Do I need a visa to travel to India?

Yes, British passport holders require a visa to enter India. The most convenient option is an e-Visa, which can be applied for online at indianvisaonline.gov.in/evisa/tvoa.html. The process is straightforward and typically takes 4–5 working days. Each family member, including children, must have their own visa. We recommend applying at least 28 days before your arrival date.

What are the passport requirements?

Your passport (and your children's) must be valid for a minimum of 6 months from the date you plan to leave India. Additionally, your passport should have at least 2 blank pages for the visa and entry/exit stamps. If your passport is expiring soon, we recommend renewing it before applying for your Indian visa. Children under 16 should have their own passport or travel document.

When is the best time to visit Kerala with children?

The best time to visit Kerala is from October to March, when the weather is cool, dry, and pleasant. This is ideal for families, and the weather is perfect for sightseeing and outdoor activities. The Southwest Monsoon (June to September) brings heavy rainfall, which can disrupt travel plans and activities. Our package excludes the peak holiday period (15 Dec – 15 Jan) due to higher prices and larger crowds.

What should I pack for Kerala?

Pack lightweight, breathable clothing suitable for a tropical climate. Include comfortable walking shoes, sandals, a light rain jacket (especially if visiting during monsoon shoulder months), sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat. For temple visits, carry a shawl or lightweight scarf to cover shoulders and knees—this applies to children too. Swimming attire is essential for beach days and some hotel pools. In Munnar, where temperatures are cooler, bring a light cardigan or sweater for the evenings. Pack any essential medications and a basic first-aid kit.

What meals are included in the package?

Daily breakfast is included at all hotels throughout your stay. Additionally, a sumptuous lunch is provided during your private houseboat day cruise at Alleppey, prepared by your private chef with fresh local ingredients. The chef can accommodate dietary preferences and children's tastes upon prior notification. Other meals (lunches and dinners) are not included, allowing you the freedom to explore local restaurants and Kerala's exceptional cuisine with the family.

Are flights included in the package price?

No, this is a land-only package and international flights are not included. We recommend booking your flights separately, arranging your own travel insurance, and arriving at Cochin Airport on Day 1. We can provide guidance on flight recommendations and arrange airport transfers as part of your package.

What activities can young children enjoy?

Children can enjoy tea plantation walks (at their own pace), bird watching at Kumarakom, the houseboat cruise (favourite with children), beach activities at Marari, and exploring local villages and markets. The Kathakali performance is visually engaging for children aged 5 and above. We customise activities based on your children's ages and interests—just let us know in advance.

What is the altitude in Munnar, and will it affect my children?

Munnar is situated at an elevation of approximately 1,600 metres (5,250 feet) above sea level. Most children experience no difficulty at this altitude, though some may feel slightly breathless during the first day. To acclimatise, drink plenty of water, rest, and avoid strenuous activity on your first day. If you have concerns about altitude, please consult your doctor before travel. We recommend extra fluids for children at higher altitude.
