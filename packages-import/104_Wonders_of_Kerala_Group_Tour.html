Why Kerala Group Tours?

Wonders of Kerala is an extended 11-day escorted group tour designed for discerning travellers who want to experience the full spectrum of Kerala's natural and cultural treasures. With a maximum of 26 guests, this immersive journey offers the perfect balance: the camaraderie of group travel combined with flexibility and the expertise of professional guides. From the historic colonial charm of Cochin to the misty tea gardens of Munnar, the wildlife-rich sanctuary of Thekkady, the serene backwaters of Alleppey with an overnight houseboat stay, the dramatic cliffs of Varkala, and the golden beaches of Kovalam, this adventure showcases every dimension of God's Own Country.

Our escorted groups benefit from an experienced tour leader, AC luxury coach transport, comprehensive meal plans (MAP and APAI), and carefully selected 4-star premium hotels. Every element is arranged—sightseeing, transfers, meals, entrance fees—so you arrive and immerse yourself in Kerala's wonders. Whether witnessing a sunrise game drive at Periyar, learning about spice plantations, watching traditional Kathakali performances, or drifting through palm-fringed backwaters on a private luxury houseboat, Wonders of Kerala transforms you into a confident explorer with new friends and unforgettable memories.

Your Day-by-Day Journey

Arrival in Cochin
DAY 1

Arrive in Cochin & Explore Fort Cochin

Welcome to Kerala! Arrive at Cochin Airport and transfer to your hotel in Fort Cochin. Settle into your accommodation and embark on a leisurely exploration of this charming historic quarter. Wander the colonial streets, discover the iconic Chinese Fishing Nets, visit local spice markets, and absorb the UNESCO World Heritage ambiance. Gather with your group for a traditional welcome dinner.

Hotel: Old Court House, Cochin
Meals: Dinner
Cochin Heritage & Drive to Munnar
DAY 2

Cochin Sightseeing & Drive to Munnar

After breakfast, your guide leads a comprehensive sightseeing tour of Cochin's heritage sites. Visit the Dutch Palace (Mattancherry Palace) with its ancient murals, the centuries-old Jewish Synagogue surrounded by spice markets, St Francis Church, and more. Enjoy lunch before boarding your AC luxury coach for the scenic drive to Munnar (140 km, approximately 4 hours). Wind through plantation-covered hills and arrive at this cool hill station for an evening at leisure.

Hotel: Edan Woods Resort, Munnar
Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Distance: 140 km / 4 hrs
Munnar Tea Plantations & Spice Gardens
DAY 3

Munnar Sightseeing

Spend a full day exploring Munnar's breathtaking landscapes with your English-speaking guide. Visit the picturesque Mattupetty Dam, experience panoramic vistas from Echo Point, climb to Rajamala—home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr—and tour a working spice garden to learn about cardamom, pepper, ginger, and nutmeg cultivation at 1,600 metres. Lunch at a local restaurant provides authentic flavours. Return to your resort as the misty evening settles around the mountains.

Hotel: Edan Woods Resort, Munnar
Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Thekkady Wildlife & Martial Arts
DAY 4

Drive to Thekkady & Periyar Sanctuary

After breakfast, board your coach for the drive to Thekkady (110 km, approximately 4 hours), home to the legendary Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary. Check into your resort and explore the sanctuary with your guide. In the evening, witness an authentic Kalaripayettu martial arts performance—an ancient Kerala fighting system—in a traditional setting, offering insight into local culture and history.

Hotel: WGH Portico, Thekkady
Meals: Breakfast, Dinner
Distance: 110 km / 4 hrs
Periyar Boat Ride & Spice Plantations
DAY 5

Periyar Game Viewing & Spice Plantation Tour

Wake early for a 6:30 AM game-viewing boat ride on Lake Periyar—a thrilling chance to spot tigers, elephants, gaur, sambar, and exotic birdlife. After breakfast, enjoy lunch at a local restaurant. In the afternoon, tour a working spice plantation to learn about cardamom, pepper, ginger, nutmeg, and other Kerala spices. In the evening, witness a mesmerizing traditional Kathakali performance, a classical Kerala art form with elaborate costumes and storytelling.

Hotel: WGH Portico, Thekkady
Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Kumarakom Lake & Bird Sanctuary
DAY 6

Drive to Kumarakom & Bird Sanctuary

After breakfast, drive to Kumarakom (150 km, approximately 4 hours), a serene destination on the banks of Vembanad Lake, Kerala's largest freshwater lake. Check into your lakeside resort and spend the afternoon exploring the Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary with your guide. Over 200 species of migratory and resident birds inhabit this paradise—Siberian cranes, herons, egrets, kingfishers. Spot them from viewing platforms as golden light dances across the water. Group meals foster camaraderie.

Hotel: Lake Song or Whispering, Kumarakom
Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Distance: 150 km / 4 hrs
Alleppey Luxury Houseboat Cruise
DAY 7

Alleppey Overnight Houseboat Experience

Transfer to Alleppey, the gateway to Kerala's famous backwaters. Board your private AC luxury houseboat for an unforgettable overnight cruise through the intricate network of lagoons, lakes, and canals. Drift past coconut palms, spice plantations, and traditional fishing villages. All meals—breakfast, lunch, and dinner—are prepared aboard by a private chef using fresh, authentic Kerala cuisine. Sleep gently to the rhythm of the backwaters, sharing this magical experience with your group.

Hotel: Luxury House Boat, Alleppey
Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner (all on board)
Varkala Beach & Red Cliffs
DAY 8

Disembark Houseboat & Drive to Varkala

After breakfast and disembark from your houseboat, drive to Varkala (140 km, approximately 4 hours), a unique beach destination famous for its dramatic palm-covered red cliffs rising above the Arabian Sea. Check into your clifftop resort and spend the afternoon exploring Varkala Beach, discovering Papanasam Beach, and visiting the sacred Janardanaswamy Temple perched above the shoreline. Relax and watch the sunset paint the sky.

Hotel: Elixir Cliff Resort, Varkala
Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Distance: 140 km / 4 hrs
Trivandrum Heritage & Kovalam Beach
DAY 9

Drive to Kovalam & Trivandrum Heritage Tour

After breakfast, drive to Kovalam (60 km, approximately 1.5 hours). En route, stop in Trivandrum for a guided heritage sightseeing tour. Visit the magnificent Padmanabhaswamy Temple—one of the world's richest temples—with its iconic gopuram, the historic Napier Museum with eclectic art collections, and the Government Art Gallery. Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant. Arrive at Kovalam in the late afternoon and check into your beachfront resort. Spend the rest of the day at leisure on the iconic crescent beach.

Hotel: Uday Samudra Beach & Leisure Resort, Kovalam
Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Distance: 60 km / 1.5 hrs
Kovalam Leisure Day
DAY 10

Full Day Leisure at Kovalam

Enjoy a full day at your beachfront resort. Relax on Kovalam Beach, swim in the Arabian Sea, participate in water sports, enjoy spa treatments, or simply unwind with a good book. Your resort offers excellent facilities and multiple dining options. Gather with your group for a farewell dinner in the evening, reflecting on the incredible journey through Kerala's wonders.

Hotel: Uday Samudra Beach & Leisure Resort, Kovalam
Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
Departure from Trivandrum
DAY 11

Transfer to Trivandrum Airport & Departure

After breakfast, transfer to Trivandrum International Airport for your onward flight. As you depart, carry with you the memories of mist-covered mountains, pristine backwaters, wildlife encounters, dramatic cliffs, golden beaches, and the friendships forged with your group. The wonders of Kerala will stay with you long after your return home.

Indian Visa Information

Who Does This Apply To?

British passport holders can apply for an Indian e-Visa online, which is the most convenient option for UK citizens travelling to India for tourism purposes. The e-Visa is a digital visa issued electronically and does not require a physical stamp in your passport.

How to Apply

Visit indianvisaonline.gov.in/evisa/tvoa.html to apply for your Indian e-Visa. Apply no earlier than 30 days before your arrival date; we recommend applying at least 28 days in advance. Ensure your passport is valid for at least 6 months from your travel date and has at least 2 blank pages.

Indian Reference Contact

When applying for your e-Visa, you may be asked for an Indian reference contact. If needed, please contact us and we can assist with providing the necessary details for your application.

Need Help with Your Visa?

While the online e-Visa process is straightforward, professional visa assistance is available through The Travel Visa Company. Please note that third-party visa fees apply if you choose this service. We recommend applying yourself first, as it is usually quicker and more cost-effective.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the group size for this tour?

Wonders of Kerala is limited to a maximum of 26 guests, ensuring a personalised experience while enjoying the benefits of group travel. The minimum group size is 15 guests. This carefully calibrated size allows you to meet fellow travellers and benefit from group facilities while maintaining an intimate, manageable atmosphere.

What is included in the overnight houseboat experience?

Your private AC luxury houseboat includes overnight accommodation with all meals (breakfast, lunch, and dinner) prepared aboard by a private chef using fresh, authentic Kerala cuisine. You'll cruise through the intricate network of backwater lagoons, lakes, and canals, passing coconut palms, spice plantations, and traditional fishing villages. This is truly the highlight of many Kerala tours.

Can I see tigers at Periyar?

Periyar is a tiger reserve, and tiger sightings are possible but not guaranteed. However, the early-morning boat ride (6:30 AM) offers excellent opportunities to see elephants, gaur, sambar, spotted deer, wild boar, and a remarkable variety of birdlife. Sightings depend on wildlife movement patterns and seasonal factors. Whether or not you see tigers, the boat ride through pristine lake scenery is unforgettable.

Can you accommodate dietary requirements?

Yes, we can accommodate most dietary requirements including vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, and other restrictions. Please inform us at the time of booking so we can notify the hotels and coordinate with the tour leader. Indian cuisine naturally includes excellent vegetarian options, and our hotel partners are experienced in catering to diverse dietary needs.

Are there single rooms available, and what is the single supplement?

While the package is based on twin/double occupancy, single rooms are available at a supplement. Please contact us for current single supplement pricing. Availability of single rooms is limited, so we recommend booking early if you require one. The houseboat accommodation is shared as designed.

What is the coach like, and how comfortable are the journeys?

Your group travels in an AC luxury 34-seater coach with a professional driver. The coach is fitted with comfortable seating, air conditioning, and amenities to ensure pleasant journeys. While some journeys span 4 hours (such as Cochin to Munnar), the scenic routes, regular rest stops, and the coach's comfort make these drives enjoyable rather than taxing.

What should I pack for a Kerala group tour?

Pack lightweight, breathable clothing suitable for a tropical climate. Include comfortable walking shoes, sandals, a light rain jacket, sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat. For temple visits, carry a shawl or lightweight scarf to cover shoulders and knees. Swimming attire is essential for beach days. In Munnar, where temperatures are cooler, bring a light cardigan for evenings.

What is the best time to travel with Wonders of Kerala?

Wonders of Kerala operates in January, when the weather is cool, dry, and perfect for sightseeing. This is the peak tourist season, and the climate is ideal for outdoor activities and exploration. Specific departure dates are available upon request. The Southwest Monsoon (June-September) is avoided, as heavy rainfall can disrupt travel plans.

Are flights included in the package price?

No, this is a land-only package and international flights are not included. You'll arrive at Cochin Airport on Day 1 and depart from Trivandrum Airport on Day 11. We recommend booking your flights separately and arranging your own travel insurance. We can provide guidance on flight recommendations and arrange airport transfers as part of your package.

What happens if I need to cancel or make changes?

We understand that circumstances change. Please contact us to discuss cancellation and amendment policies, which vary depending on how far in advance you cancel. Travel insurance is highly recommended to protect yourself against unforeseen circumstances. Our team will work with you to find the best solution.
