Indian Visa Information

Who Does This Apply To?

British passport holders can apply for an Indian e-Visa online, which is the most convenient option for UK citizens travelling to India for medical or tourism purposes. The e-Visa is a digital visa issued electronically and does not require a physical stamp in your passport.

How to Apply

Visit indianvisaonline.gov.in/evisa/tvoa.html to apply for your Indian e-Visa. Apply no earlier than 30 days before your date of arrival; we recommend applying at least 28 days in advance to allow processing time. Ensure your passport is valid for at least 6 months from your date of travel and has at least 2 blank pages.

Medical Travel Visa

Your e-Visa application can be submitted under the tourism category. Upon request, we can provide documentation from the hospital confirming your medical appointment, which may support your visa application if required.

Need Help with Your Visa?

While the online e-Visa process is straightforward, professional visa assistance is available through The Travel Visa Company. Please note that third-party visa fees apply if you choose this service. We recommend applying yourself first, as it is usually quicker and more cost-effective.

Frequently Asked Questions

How are the hospitals accredited, and are they recognised internationally?

All hospitals partnered with Ashfield Travel for Package 116 are accredited by international bodies including the National Accreditation Board for Hospitals & Healthcare Providers (NABH), Joint Commission International (JCI), and other international quality standards. These facilities meet rigorous safety, hygiene, and clinical excellence standards equivalent to Western hospitals. Many of our partner doctors have additional qualifications from UK, US, or European institutions.

Will there be translation support during my health check-up?

Yes. Your Ashfield Travel local coordinator will accompany you to all medical appointments and consultations. All hospitals have English-speaking staff, and professional medical translation is available as needed. Your coordinator will ensure clear communication between you and your healthcare providers throughout your programme.

What happens if my health check-up reveals a serious issue requiring treatment?

If additional treatment is recommended beyond your selected health check-up programme, you have several options. You can proceed with treatment in India at the hospital, arrange specialist consultations for a treatment plan to be implemented back in the UK, or request your complete medical records and reports to share with your home healthcare provider. Your Ashfield Travel coordinator will discuss options with you and support whatever decision you make.

Can I combine my health check-up with leisure time in Kerala?

Absolutely. Many clients extend their stay to enjoy Kerala's natural beauty and wellness attractions. We can arrange transfers to beach resorts, backwater houseboat cruises, Ayurvedic spa treatments, or guided nature tours at additional cost. Your health programme typically occupies 3–7 days, leaving flexibility to combine medical care with relaxation.

Is travel insurance required, and does it cover medical tourism?

Travel insurance is highly recommended for all international travel. Most standard UK travel insurance policies cover medical tourism for planned procedures and health assessments. We recommend reviewing your policy and confirming coverage before booking. Some insurers specialise in medical tourism travel insurance. Ashfield Travel cannot provide insurance advice, but your insurer can confirm what is covered.

Will my home doctor accept the medical reports from India?

Yes. All medical reports are provided in English and follow international standards recognised by UK healthcare providers. Reports include complete diagnostic findings, specialist assessments, and recommendations. Your home doctor can review these reports and use them for ongoing management of your health. If your doctor requires any additional information or clarification, our hospital partners can provide further documentation.

Can I arrange follow-up consultations remotely after returning to the UK?

Yes. Many of our partner hospitals offer telemedicine consultations for follow-up care after you return home. Your specialist can discuss test results, answer questions, and monitor your progress via video consultation. This service is available at an additional cost and can be arranged through Ashfield Travel or directly with your hospital.

What visa type do I need for medical tourism?

British passport holders typically apply for a Tourism e-Visa (short-term), which is valid for 60 days and costs approximately GBP 22. Some applicants with longer stays may opt for a longer-validity visa. Your medical appointment letter from the hospital can support your visa application. We can provide guidance on visa requirements during the booking process.

Are there any health requirements or vaccinations I need before travelling to India?

Consult your GP or a travel clinic at least 6–8 weeks before your trip. Common recommendations for India include vaccinations for hepatitis A, typhoid, and polio booster, depending on your individual medical history and immunity status. You may also wish to discuss malaria prophylaxis, depending on regions you plan to visit. Your local NHS travel clinic can provide personalised advice based on your health profile.

Can I bring a family member or companion to support me?

Yes. We encourage companions to join you for support during your health programme. Companion accommodation can be arranged at your hotel at an additional cost. Companions can attend consultations with you for translation and support, and Ashfield Travel can include them in daily itineraries and leisure activities. This is particularly appreciated by many clients undergoing significant health assessments.

What if I need to postpone or reschedule my health check-up?

Programme dates can be adjusted based on hospital availability and your personal circumstances. We recommend as much notice as possible for changes. Cancellation and rescheduling terms are clearly outlined in your booking documentation. Travel insurance may cover certain cancellations, so review your policy. Contact Ashfield Travel immediately if your circumstances change.

Is it safe to travel to India for medical treatment?

India, particularly Kerala, is widely regarded as safe for medical tourism by international health and safety organisations. Millions of international patients travel to India annually for medical care. Cochin is a major healthcare hub with excellent infrastructure, security, and hospitality services. As with all international travel, standard sensible precautions apply. Consult the UK Foreign Office for latest safety advice.
