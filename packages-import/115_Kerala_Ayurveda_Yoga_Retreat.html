Transform Through Ancient Wisdom

Discover the profound healing power of Ayurveda in this 14-day intensive wellness retreat nestled on Cherai Beach in Kerala. This is not simply a holiday—it is a journey of deep transformation guided by the time-tested principles of Ayurveda, India's ancient system of medicine and wellness. Over two weeks, you will immerse yourself in daily therapies, personalised treatments, and mindful practices designed to restore balance, rejuvenate your body, and clarify your mind.

Each guest arrives as an individual with unique constitutional needs. On Day 1, our experienced Ayurvedic physician will conduct a detailed assessment of your Prakriti—your natural body constitution—and design a completely personalised treatment plan for the remaining 13 days. You will experience authentic Ayurvedic therapies including Abhyanga (oil massage), Shirodhara (calming oil treatment for the mind), Panchakarma (deep detoxification), and gentle to dynamic yoga practices. Combined with nourishing Ayurvedic meals prepared according to your body type, daily meditation, and the tranquil beachside setting, this retreat offers the ideal environment for healing and spiritual growth. Return home renewed, balanced, and equipped with the wisdom to maintain your wellness journey.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Ayurveda?

Ayurveda is an ancient system of medicine originating from India over 5,000 years ago. It views health as a balance between three fundamental energies—Vata (movement), Pitta (transformation), and Kapha (stability)—that exist in all people. Illness occurs when these energies become imbalanced. Ayurvedic treatments use herbs, oils, dietary changes, lifestyle practices, and therapies to restore harmony and prevent disease. It is a holistic science of living well, not merely treating disease.

What should I expect from Panchakarma therapy?

Panchakarma is the most intensive Ayurvedic detoxification protocol, translating to "five actions." Treatments may include Virechana (herbal purification of the digestive system) or Basti (therapeutic enemas using medicinal oils and herbs). These therapies aim to eliminate deep-rooted toxins and restore balance at the cellular level. Not all guests require Panchakarma—your physician will assess your needs on Day 1. Those who receive it experience it on Day 7, with a rest day following. Side effects are minimal when performed by trained professionals in a proper setting.

Are there dietary restrictions I should be aware of?

All meals at the retreat are vegetarian and prepared according to Ayurvedic principles specific to your body constitution. If you have food allergies or intolerances, please inform us at the time of booking so we can ensure appropriate meals. Alcohol and tobacco are not permitted at the retreat. All food is freshly prepared using organic ingredients where possible, supporting optimal healing and nutrition.

Do I need to be physically fit to attend?

No. This retreat welcomes people of all fitness levels and ages. Yoga and movement practices are adapted to your individual capacity—you will never be pushed beyond your comfort. The programme emphasises gentle, restorative practices alongside more dynamic sessions. If you have any health concerns or physical limitations, please inform us before arrival so we can tailor your yoga and therapy sessions accordingly.

Who is this retreat suitable for?

This retreat is designed for anyone seeking genuine wellness transformation and healing. It suits those experiencing stress, fatigue, chronic conditions, or simply anyone wanting to prevent illness and live more healthfully. It is also ideal for those interested in exploring Eastern wellness philosophies deeply. People with serious medical conditions should consult their doctor before attending and inform us in advance. Pregnant women should also consult their healthcare provider first.

Can I combine this retreat with Kerala sightseeing?

The focus of this programme is deep healing and wellness; sightseeing would distract from your transformation. However, you could easily extend your trip before or after the 14-day retreat to explore Kerala's attractions—backwaters, beaches, tea plantations, cultural sites—independently or through a separate tour. Cherai Beach itself is beautiful for peaceful walks and nature connection. We recommend dedicating the full 14 days to wellness, then exploring at your own pace if desired.

What is the weather like in Kerala during my visit?

Kerala's weather varies by season. October to March is the dry season with pleasant temperatures (25-32°C) and low humidity—the ideal time to visit. April to May is hot (32-35°C). June to September is the monsoon season with heavy rainfall, lush landscapes, and cooler temperatures. The retreat operates year-round, but the October-March period is most comfortable for guests. The beachside location at Cherai ensures coastal breezes throughout the year.

What should I pack for the retreat?

Pack lightweight, loose-fitting clothing suitable for tropical weather and yoga practice. Include swimwear, a light rain jacket, comfortable walking shoes, sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat. For yoga, bring any personal props you prefer (though these are provided). Toiletries are supplied, but bring personal items you prefer. Modest clothing is respectful during cultural activities. The retreat centre is simple but clean and designed for wellness—avoid heavy makeup or perfumes. A journal can be helpful for reflecting during your journey.
